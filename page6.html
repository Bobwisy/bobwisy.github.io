Setting Up A Company In South Africa

When a decision is made to set up a company, certain questions often come to mind. The first 2 being:

Thanks for filling out the form!

• How do I get the company up in the quickest and most cost-effective way possible?

• Do I need the assistance of a lawyer with the setup?  Getting a company set up goes beyond just registering the company. The three initial steps that go hand-in-hand are: 
• Company registration,
• Opening of a bank account, and
• Tax registration.

The first thing to consider with the set up of a company should not be how quickly the set up can be done but rather what kind of company needs to be registered to meet the needs of the incorporator. Conducting some research might help you get the answers you need if you have the time, but talking to a profession will definitely get you the answers you need and save you precious time.
On deciding on what type of company to register, the next step to consider is how to have the company set up and what documentations are required. The Companies and Intellectual Property Commission (“CIPC”) has streamlined the company registration process to enable anyone registers their company without the need of a third party. They have also provided standard form Memorandum of Incorporations (MOI) so an incorporator does not necessary have to get one prepared for them.


Making use of the standard forms of the CIPC may be considered by most as simplifying the process but care must be taken when there are two or more people or businesses involved as incorporators or shareholders. In this case, it is advisable to have a customised Shareholders Agreement and MOI prepared to govern the relationship between or among parties.
It must always be remembered that standard forms are called by their names for a reason because they are generic and are not tailored to cover any particular person’s interests or capture agreements reached by the parties involved.
Therefore to answer the 2 questions posed at the beginning of this article; The quickest and most cost effective way is to engage the process by yourself keeping in mind that you may miss certain nuances that you may need to attend to and only an experienced professional will be able to point out these nuances. If missed, the process may end up taking longer and become more costly in the future.
Ideally, you should consult with a lawyer on the type of company you want to set up to ensure it is the right vehicle required and especially so if there is more than one party as incorporator or shareholder.
